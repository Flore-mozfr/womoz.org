====== Other women's groups ======
[[http://www.devchix.com/|DevChix]]: international group of female programmers.\\
[[http://groups.drupal.org/drupalchix|Drupal Chix]]: if you are a woman who needs some help/guidance in order to get (more) involved with Drupal and the Drupal community, who wants to network and get to know other women involved in Drupal or anyone who has ideas on things we can do to help encourage women to participate in Drupal ... then this is the group for you.\\
[[http://geekspeakr.com/|GeekSpeakr]]: website to help technical women speakers and event organizers to find each other.\\
[[http://www.phpwomen.org/|PHP Women]]: user group specifically targeted toward the women in the PHP world.\\
[[http://ubuntu-women.org|Ubuntu Women]]: team functioning under Ubuntu to provide a platform and encouragement for women to contribute to Ubuntu-Linux, a Debian based free and open-source GNU/Linux software.\\
[[http://women.debian.org/home/|Debian Women]]: seeking to balance and diversify the Debian Project by actively engaging with interested women and encouraging them to become more involved with Debian.\\
[[http://live.gnome.org/GnomeWomen|GNOME Women]]: GNOME Women is a group dedicated to providing encouragement for women to contribute to GNOME, a free and open-source Linux/Unix desktop suite.\\
[[http://geekfeminism.wikia.com/wiki/Geek_Feminism_Wiki|Geek Feminism]]: the Geek Feminism Wiki is a resource for and about women in geek communities.\\
[[http://anitaborg.org/initiatives/systers|Systers]]: Systers is the world’s largest email community of technical women in computing. It promotes the interests of women in the computing and technology fields.\\
[[http://www.anitaborg.org/|Anita Borg Institute for Women and Technology]]: "We are women technologists. We use technology to connect our communities. We create technology because it is who we are — intelligent, creative and driven (...) Working with men that believe in our mission, **we are changing the world for women and technology**."\\
